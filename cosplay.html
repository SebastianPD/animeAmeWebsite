Cosplay at Anime Ame

Cosplay is one of the highlights of any anime convention, and Anime Ame is no exception! We love seeing the incredible creativity, craftsmanship, and passion that our attendees bring to their costumes. Whether you're debuting an elaborate build or rocking a casual closet cosplay, vibe out, our convention floor is your stage! 

Cosplay Contest — Sunday, May 10

The Ichigo Ichiban Cosplay Contest is the main event! Take the stage and compete for prizes in front of our expert panel of judges and a cheering audience.

Schedule Saturday May 9th

  • 11:00 AM — Contest Pre-Judging (Room 403)

Schedule Sunday May 10th

  • 1:00 PM — Contest check-in (Backstage Area)
  • 2:00 PM — Beginners Contest begins (Main Stage)
  • 2:30 PM — Journeyman Contest begins (Main Stage)
  • 3:00 PM — Masters Contest begins (Main Stage)
  • 3:30 PM — Anime Ame Trivia Show
  • 4:00 PM — Awards ceremony

Categories for each experience level

  • Best Sewist — Judged on sewing quality, detail, technique, and patterning
  • Best Crafter — Judged on craftsmanship, detail, and technique
  • Best in Show — The overall top entry combining craftsmanship and performance
  • Fan Favorite — Voted on by the audience!

Rules

  • All ages and skill levels are welcome to compete
  • Costumes must be from an anime, manga, Japanese video game, or Japanese pop culture source
  • Props must be convention-safe (no real weapons, sharp edges, or projectiles)
  • Pre-recorded audio for walk-ons must be submitted by 11:59 PM on May 5th

Accessibility

The contest stage is fully accessible with ramp access. If you need any accommodations to participate — extended time, mobility assistance, sensory considerations, or anything else — please contact us at cosplay@animeame.com.

Cosplay Guidelines & Policies

To keep everyone safe and comfortable, please follow these guidelines when cosplaying at Anime Ame:

  • Costumes must be family-friendly — If we get notified by multiple people about your cosplay we have a cosplay council who will look at your cosplay and decide if you can stay at the venue. General rule of thumb is if your costume ends up flashing someone, or If you are dressed like a pre cyborg Stroheim from JJBA with all the historical symbols we will be asking you to leave.
  • Props must be peace-bonded — Visit our prop check station at registration to get your props approved and tagged
  • No real weapons — Realistic-looking prop weapons must be clearly fake. Metal weapons, even decorative ones, are not permitted
  • Cosplay is not consent — Always ask before touching someone's costume or taking their photo
  • Keep pathways clear — Large costumes and props should not block hallways or doorways
  • Changing areas — Designated cosplay changing rooms are available near the registration area
